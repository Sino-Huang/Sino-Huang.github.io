Key components#

Model#

Adapter Style (with Risks and Paradox)

  • Hierarchical structure of the policy architecture (ref: Drexler, Dominik, Jendrik Seipp, and Hector Geffner. “Learning hierarchical policies by iteratively reducing the width of sketch rules.” Proceedings of the International Conference on Principles of Knowledge Representation and Reasoning. Vol. 19. No. 1. 2023.)

  • forming a tree-like decision hierarchy.

  • Example: Top level, abstract policy with few focusing features; low levels are the detailed policy with more focusing features

  • Gated Residual Connections: Enable direct control from abstract high-level policies to low-level actions, bypassing intermediate layers.

  • The Paradox: Low-Level Parameter Bloat: In this adapter style, low-level modules (e.g., robotic manipulation) would require more parameters than high-level modules (low-level policy = base parameters + parent’s adapter’s parameters + its own adapter’s parameters.) In contrast, high-level policies process abstract semantics with smaller parameter counts.

VLM style

  • End-to-End Integration Uses a single VLM:
    1. Understand multimodal inputs (visual scenes + language instructions).
    2. Determine the current task mode (e.g., which general policy to use).
    3. Generate corresponding actions directly.
  • Challenge
    • this is no more a VLA style, requiring extra methods to map the output to the action space.

VLA style*

  • Could be the best fit

  • it seems that the randomness (noise Gaussian) can be the perfect fit for generating a variety of solutions (promote exploration)
