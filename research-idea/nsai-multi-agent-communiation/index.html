Background

In multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) and robotic coordination, the trade-off between instruction granularity and communication constraints represents a central research challenge. At its core, this trade-off involves balancing the level of detail in instructions against limitations in communication bandwidth, latency, and robustness to noise.

  • More detailed instructions can potentially enable more precise coordination but require higher bandwidth and may be more vulnerable to communication failures.
  • Conversely, abstract instructions require less bandwidth and might be more robust to noise or delays, but may leave more room for interpretation or error during execution.

The optimal level of instruction abstraction may depend on various factors, such as task complexity or even the intelligence of the collaborating agent.

Therefore, it is important to investigate the most effective communication strategy in a master‚Äìfollower setting, where the master agent leads the Overcooked mission and issues instructions to guide the follower in coordinating with them.
