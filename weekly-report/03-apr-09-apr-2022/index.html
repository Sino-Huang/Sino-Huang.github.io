Collect Demonstrations#

The NetHack challenge report mentioned that none of the agents succeed the game (Ascension)

Ascension is too difficult to reach, and the agents that are driven by the in-game scores may still have no clues how to win the game.

Therefore we may provide demonstrations to agents for behaviour cloning.

Some other challenges in NetHack#

Each starting combination will alter the game experience, and thus impact the difficulty of the game and the most suitable strategy. For example, wizards start with magic and magical items, while rangers begin with a bow and arrow; elves are generally intelligent whereas dwarves are strong.

For the purposes of the NetHack challenge, the character is randomised during evaluation for the competition, so it is likely wise to consider agents that can perform well across a variety of hero configurations.

This is not our focus for now.

Consider this challenging case#

you have such an advice in the NetHack Guidebook that

“One advice for food: Don’t eat anything your pet won’t. It’ll keep you out of the worst trouble.”

So, when you want to eat an edible item, you need to recall whether your pet ate this item before, and then lowering the probability of eating the item if your pet rarely ate it.

the challenging part is that

  • The agent has to recall all the memories related to the topic “pet eating food”.
    • how to store the memory
    • how to retrieve the memory
  • How to make a judgement (i.e., based on the memory, the agent concludes that our pet rarely ate this item)
